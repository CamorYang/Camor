Less willingness to pay for Lemonbox

Lemonbox is a personalized vitamin product. Unlike the traditional way to get extra nutrition after complex research on health and different nutrient supplements, the users only need to fill the easy questionnaires by Lemonbox and they would get everything they need within a package. It’s a great innovation for that many people nowadays are disturbed with the complicated combination that has to be completed by themselves’s research.

But since many people are attracted to purchase it, they seldom buy it again. As a result , we did the research to understand the cause behind it.

under the guidance of Yushi Wang from UXOFFER and Xiaoyan Song from Lemonbox, I led the research and cooperated with Shuozhi Shen, Yue Xu, Rongxiang Rao to do this design research.

Better health with more convenient service

Since the establishment, Lemonbox has designed the easy questionnaire to reduce people’s burden from researching health. To have a knowledge of their health situation, they just fill the Lemonbox questionnaire and get a health evaluation, based on the evaluation, they could know which nourishment are suitable to them.

With the completion of questionnaires, Lemonbox provides them with the box, one pocket per day, to make it easy for users to take.

It has become a new product perfectly fills the gap of traditional tonics.

Early insights from our experience

As we have experienced it, we noticed that the full process of taking Lemonbox could be divided into different parts for that the users would be attracted for certain reason and consider whether to have it. After they purchased it, the process began and next decision would be made by them.

1 Life normalcy & Health Situation

Our life would be under certain situation that requires the help from tonics to supplement necessary nutrition.

2 Purchase decision

After seeing the questionnaire results, we would decide on whether to purchase a Lemonbox, since it’s our first time to attempt it.

3 The 1st Lemonbox month

During the process, we would be trying to keep taking Lemonbox regularly, despite some interruption.

4 Repurchase consideration

After we have finished it, we would consider whether to repurchase it to go on. The consideration are complex and personal.

5 Repurchase loss

Since the process of taking Lemonbox is tiny, we have no impression on the influence Lemonbox brought to us. Therefore, we decided not to repurchase it.

Long-term unchangeable lifestyle

They are suffering from burdened life, with much coursework or report to finish. Hence, even in the midnight they would have coffee and excels together. Sometimes due to the urgent time limit, they reverse their day and night with no regular work and rest. It’s the long-term situation.

universal and chronic symptoms

Under this life situation, their body becomes weak gradually. They have increasingly serious problems such as eyestrain, headache, anxiety , sleeplessness which are changing their life without being noticed.

accidental and intense symptoms

Meanwhile, due to the unhealthy lifestyle, they would suffer from sudden gastrointestinal discomfort or alopecia. The intense pain or symptom would catch their attention and at this time, they reach out for Lemonbox and hope to get their expected effect-- to cure their symptoms.

Deeper insights

Disappearance of service and favorability

With their first purchase and attempt, consumers began to test whether Lemonbox was efficient and suitable to them. But the expectation was wrongly placed.

Due the the limited insights we drew from the first version of the user research, we iterated on the content of user research and found some more problems that leads to the disappearance of their preference toward Lemonbox.

gap between 1 month and course of treatment

Lemonbox standard version, the first choice for consumers’ attempt, is for 1 month per package. But the course of treatment for the symptoms is complicated, which are influenced by many factors such as life habits or physical fitness.

Despite Lemonbox providing both relief and cure for chronic and intense symptoms respectively, they only care about intense symptoms, which are opposite to their lifestyle, indicating the symptoms are universal and hard to change.

visualizations of course of treatment made by me

Preference for cure

Are my symptoms removed? Consumers complain that Lemonbox didn’t remove their issues within a month, although they didn’t notice how much has the symptoms been relieved. They mistake Lemonbox as a medicine to offset the bad conditions they confronted.

Expectation gap

The role Lemonbox plays in consumers’ life is health care products, while people expect Lemonbox to cure their symptom within 1 month, and this fail their expectation.

Integrated research

Costly Decision

As everyone finished their first month’s attempt, they all need to decide whether to repurchase it. But such a decision is costly, since many factors influence people’s thinking whether it worth repurchase.

pic made by me

As we have did the user research and secondary research, we got to know the real cost for decision.

Interruption of taking medicine

According to the survey, an accustomed habit would be forgotten after pause for 7 days. Corona Virus caused the delay of delivery. Therefore , due to the long waiting they have experienced in the first time, they are unhurried to repurchase it, which led to their forgetting this habit.

Price-Health relationship

Consumers tend to accept more expensive Lemonbox when they are under serious condition, while care more about the price when they are good. They weight the price based on their health. If they are in desparate need of better health, they would be somewhat blind to price.

Hesitation from effects

When they look back on their progress, they just care about whether they are better, while the effects are displayed as maintaining conditions under overburdened work or stress. Lack of obvious effects makes them give up repurchase.

Reframing the problem

Static service increase the cost of decision making for Lemonbox

The service provided by Lemonbox,including the questionnaire, evaluation and tonic box, was static for that no subsequent interaction and feedback was avaiable during the process of taking it. This made users have little impression on its influence on their health, and subsequently led to the heavy consideration for repurchase because they don’t know what has Lemonbox brought to them.

A dynamic service

Based on our insights, we want a new Lemonbox could provide users with dynamic and everlasting service, which means that the questionnaire-based evaluation not only exists at the beginning but goes along with the whole process of taking Lemonbox to make them realize the influence of Lemonbox.

Keep the service, keep the favorability

We try to make consumers always perceive the existence of service through iteration on interfaces.

Therefore, we proposed weekly questionnaires to get feedback from consumers. In turn, we give them weekly report based on their life habits, balance between work and rest to give them suggestions continually during their processing of taking it.

Reselection of Algorithm-based questions

Since the original questionnaires are designed to get quick health analysis through AI, we selected some questions that have short-term changeable situations to utilize algorithm compatibility and engineering efficiency.

Pay for health, not for Lemonbox

To enhance their perception of connection between Lemonbox and health, we try to avoid leaving consumers impression of product sales.

We turned the page for purchase into health schedule. Every time they consider whether to repurchase or not, they would be presented with the next month’s expected compensation from it, such as offset for 1 week’s staying up or 3 indigestion resulting from night snack.

From Static to dynamic

More questionnaire, more feedback

We optimized the flow to turn the service process from static to dynamic.

New flow for weekly questionnaires

Also, since we want to follow the original algorithm, we modify the questionnaire content: The original questionnaire contains 4 parts:

1. basic information: your weight, height, age and so on which are stable and predictable

2. nutrition target: the areas that you are going to focus on, like eyes, stomach depression or skin, it changes according to your long-term situation.

3. life habit: what you are accustomed to and actually do in your life, this would have some changes during your life, and different time have different life situations.

4. body condition: allergy, medical history and so on.This part is in line with your body, which vary frequently during your life.

collect one part, generate the whole part

we include only the life habit and body condition within the weekly questionnaire to reduce the disturbance from the long process of completing the questionnaire. Therefore,users only need to answer around 10 questions per week to generate the weekly change on their health.

Changes make sense to the final evaluation

We base the final evaluation, when you have finished the first Lemonbox, on the weekly change of questionnaire. Although they don’t know whether they have improvement, they would know the compensation of Lemonbox for their unhealthy lifestyle such as staying up late, stomach burden from midnight snack.
