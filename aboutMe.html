About Me
I am a second year Master’s student at Georgia Tech majoring in Digital Media, under School of Literature, Media, and Communication, supervised by Professor Michael Nitsche. I also work with Professor HyunJoo Oh and Noura Howell.

I used to work as a user research, designer, technologist focusing on applying wide-ranging design through ethnographic insights on tangible and embodied interaction.

My research vision lies on interweaving embodiment and agents for interpersonal dynamics to augment human on an emotional level. As human always relief social dilemma through another “speaking for us”, we perform a separated agency to resolve this issue. Agents provide potential pathway to augment such situation. Specifically, I aim to take agent as a second self to speak for human’s hesitation. I do not limit the augmentation within verbal interaction. Instead, I design embodiment of agents to build nonverbal communication with counterparts, and integrate trustworthy exbodiment with owners. I dive into how to design such embodiment, exbodiment as a second self, and how would augmented human realize the blended control, intelligence, and perceived agency with the agents.
