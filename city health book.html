Elderly fighting against Cardiovascular diseases

Cardiovascular diseases have become a pressing problem for the elderly in China. Since every service are under digital transformation, they don’t know how to get every service they need. The CVDs, along with many challenges, become a threat to them.

I worked along with Aosha Long, Hanwen Zhang to design a new way to give the elderly in China access to fight against CVDs in a more thorough way.

This work was supervised and sponsored by DESIS NETWORK, Tsinghua University, Hunan University and Jiangnan University collaboratively.

2021.2.10 - 2021.4.30 

Zero to One

Design for convenience

Cardiovascular diseases is killing people. Patients with CVDs in China are 330 million in number, 1 people out of 4 is suffering from such diseases. hypertension consists of 245 million; Stroke consists of 13 million; coronary heart diseases consist of 11 million; cor pulmonale consists of 5 million; cardiac failure consists of 8.9 million; congenital heart disease consists of 2.5 million; lower extremity diseases consist of 45.3 million.

It has become the leading cause of deaths globally. Despite the most state-of-the-art treatment, more than half of patients lose their self-care ability. Therefore, we tried to explore if there’s anything we can do to help relieve such diseases.

In China, great digital changes have begun. Many medical services have been transferred online. People register and consult with their phones for convenience to quickly grasp their conditions.

However, the elderly in China is the exception. They haven’t experienced digital social revolution When they are young, and when they are old and have access to mobile phones and laptops, their cognition decline make them have difficulty mastering mobile phones and programs. Hence, access to medical services has become a challenging problem for them.

The digital convenience has become a digital gap for the elderly.

Services online by Dingxiang Doctor, a popular medical app in China

Challenge

Fight against CVDs thoroughly and easily

Our vision for this project was to make the elderly easily fight against their CVD problems. Since these illnesses are challenging and hard to handle, we have to think of some really useful ways to tackle all the potential frustration and difficulties when they are faced with CVD threat .

Principles to disease solution is clear:

1. no offense to patients’ conditions.

2. easy access to all the preventions.

3. More thorough way to make them perpetually deal with it.

Early insights from the field

By the investigations, we have found some key factors that really exacerbated the severity of these diseases.

Pieces of research from doctor

Primary prevention & teritary prevention

According to doctors’ statement, kernel of prevention is primary prevention that intervening before health effects occur. Compared to teritary prevention, primary one could postpone the paroxysm 30-50 years later.Primary prevention includes execrises.

Gap between profession and amateur

The effects and consequence are different between doctor’s and patients’ perspectives. While the doctors would be reminded continuously by their knowledge on it, the elderly don’t know what stands in front of them when they are doing something unhealthy.

Discovery

Lost prevention

Since the most efficient way to deal with CVD problem is to make preventions, why do people still neglect or fail it?

After iteration of semi-structured interview, we researched again on the obstructons challenging their taking actions. We interviewed the doctors, patients adn relatives again to better understand their attitudes

Interview

We have found that there are some potential obstacles standing on their way to prevention.

Trust toward peers, Distrust toward doctors

Due to limited education, Chinese elderly are always frightened of doctors’ complicated description. Hence, they tend to be reserved toward their advices. Also, wardmates’ recovery directly inform them the effects of wardmates’ measures and they would follow it.

Frustration & Lack of Self-discipline

Elderly’s weakened body makes them focus more on avoid being worse instead of being better, for that better means more vigor devotion and this cost them energy. Therefore, with few devotion, they couldn’t establish long-term excution and self-discipline to improve their health sustainably.

Shame from illness and diseases

From what patients’s relatives and doctors have described,Patients are shamed of illness, when others inform them of the illness, they tend to avoid getting medical advice from professional medical services whose diagnosis are authoritative that make them at a loss.

Digital Gap

In China, services are turned online and offline service are simplified or cancelled. Smart phones’ interaction is strange to Chinese elderly since they didn’t use it until getting old. They can’t remember the interfaces and this brings them inconvenience.

New Social Contact

Losing social contact, elderly patients reach others in some public places. Take a park as an example, an elderly takes care if anyone’s strange in their body balance to find out someone with similar problems. Then he would reach out and build a connection after encounters.

Media Reliance

In China, elderly are used to note some tips on paper or use calendaries as memo, which are shouldered by app. Their accustomed materials & files are presented mainly on papers. Therefore, they have been accustomed to the paper medium

Reframing the problem

How to tackle every seemingly difficult problem?

Lack of medium-friendly measures obstructed their promotive and sustainable solutions to fight against long-term chronic CVDs. A good prevention circulation was established by both personal habit cultivation and artifact feature of being easy and friendly.

Based on this , we generated some opportunity areas to support upcoming concept

Opportunity areas

A new service for elderly CVD fighting

City health book

We are looking for a new way to make CVD elderly easily tackle this problem. City health book gives people convenient ways to access multiple services with their integrated coupon and stamps, and by turning the coupons into stamps through exchange, they would cultivate a habit of collection.

City health book

Archetype

Recognize each other

We are looking for a new way to make CVD elderly easily tackle this problem. City health book gives people convenient ways to access multiple services with their integrated coupon and stamps, and by turning the coupons into stamps through exchange, they would cultivate a habit of collection.

Exchange for service

As a result, we designed the book to make them have access to different services by papers. It’s the most familiar way since before digital age, they use paper money to do all the transactions offline, and this newly-designed rewarded coupon acts as elderly’s paper money.

Collection after consumption

We integrate some necessary services which are helpful to their health into the stamp coupons. People use coupons in exchange for services like the nutritional vegetables package and get stamps in return. Due to the collection property of stamps , they would be promoted to exchange more services and get more stamps. Also , coupons are substitute of prescription that shift their attention from shame and worry of illness to better habits.

Better Visual style

We have designed some new visual style that are familiar to the elderly to get their best experience.

Iteration for better

How we got there

There are many things to consider before the final design, we proposed 4 opportunity areas that we must follow to provide a better user experience.

Opportunity areas

New Media

Since they can’t handle smart phones well to access different services. They need to be offered a new user-friendly way to get help. A familiar way to get access is easier to be accepted by them

Shared recognition

People recognize each other’s CVDs by behaviors. To help them more sensitive and build a stronger connection, it’s necessary that we unify some logos or forms that make them connected at sight of the signs.

Collection joy

Due to poverty , Chinese elderly tend to collect something that has no use. Collection means storage and this gives them a sense of safety that when in need in the future they could tackle it with the useless collections. Coupons, tickets and stubs have priority of their collection.

Focus on life & dismiss illness

The process of living a better life has no direct relation to specific illness such as CVDs. As a result, we avoid mentioning illness during the service practices to make them participate actively with no worry.

Design Iterations

We iterated on the design concept for several times , from a health watch to an application optimized for accessibility, to get the best process. All of the past iterations have several limitations and the iterations has led us to better understanding towards this problem.

Smart watches and apps

We explored on the material design and some other design principles, finding that although some attentions would be paid according to the elderly's physiological properties, the root of their difficulty with apps and smart devices is that they can't get accustomed to their interaction for that the abstracted icon and button are designed to be actively perceived, while the elderly could only search their cognition to understand match the interaction with their past familiarity.

Concept Details

arranged flow for habit cultivation

To get benefit from this service, the patients just need to follow the flow, and get everything beneficial to their health improvement.

Health Lecture for Elderly

The elderly attend at the lectures held by general doctors and hospitals to get essential knowledge

Coupon for fitness

After the lectures, the elderly would be presented with some new coupons in exchange for some fitness to help them improve their fitness situations

Coupon for physical examination

Regular physical examinations are necessary. They could access the examinations to keep track of their health situation in time.

Co-place for food

Since diet is an essential way for them to keep their body situation in control, a coupon for quota food is great.

Co-place for sharing

Recommended place for meeting would offer them an opportunity to have connections and meet with each other

Collection Joy

After exchanging the coupons for food, physical examinations and fitness, they would get stamps in return and collect it for joy and social enjoyment when showing them off to others.

Feature 

How we ensure the cycle of this service

It's obvious that this service connect the elderly with the market, fitness rooms and some medical resources. How to support the service and avoid much cost is important for us to make sure this service is sustainable and economically-friendly.

Therefore, we tried to integrate the promotive mechanism designed for the elderly and the knowledge increment to have a positive feedback cycle.

Positive feedback cycle

By our redesign of different stakeholders’ responsibility and collaboration, we proposed a new cycle for them to generate sustainable and positive feedbacks.

Economically Sustainable

We rethink on the structure of the social finance shift to come up with a new way to make this process sustainable.

As we have discovered, the finance shift from tertiary prevention to primary prevention has been is the key factor to improve efficiency of medical resources.

Therefore, we proposed a design that support the service by finance shift.

Preventive Cycle

Low-Cost media

to reduce the cost of this cycle, we found that  the material and connection could be optimized to get better results.

Paper is low-cost. Therefore, the service’s change or alteration relies mostly on the balance between government and service providers.
