Cardiovascular Diseases is killing people.

City Health Book is a book-based service for elderly’s prevention of cardiovascular diseases. It integrates services within stamps and books to make every Chinese elderly have friendly access to prevention.

 ·   3 Million People in China died from cardiovascular diseases annually

 ·   330 million people in China are diagnosed with CVDs.

 ·   15 million  are dying from CVDs annually.

What actually led to their circumstance?

We have user research on stakeholders to examine the real cause of such a large proportion. We talked with doctors, patients, and patients’ relatives to have a complete picture of the review of patients’ past.

"The biggest obstacle to prevention is that people wouldn’t connect their daily life to illnss, for that they have no positive significant feedback. "

"Most of patients have suffered from the illness for long before going to hospital. The time that they reached out for hospital’s help has far beyond the best time for treatment."

"Doctors could do little to patients. We could just help."

"It doesn’t mean that preventive measures could prevent it, But patients could postpone their breaking out, say, from their 50 to their 90."

"Some patients don’t trust us due to their cognitive limitations."

"They don’t care their life, they don’t pay attention to it until they are obviously something wrong."

Primary prevention & teritary prevention

According to doctors’ statement, kernel of prevention is primary prevention that intervening before health effects occur. Compared to teritary prevention, primary one could postpone the paroxysm 30-50 years later.Primary prevention includes execrises.

What on earth leads to neglect and failure of prevention

After iteration of semi-structured interview, we researched again on the obstructons challenging their taking actions. We interviewed 3 doctors, 4 patients and 3 patients’ relatives

“No patience, only frustration. Elderly with CVDs have no obvious motivation to maintain such rigorous discipline.”—— Doctors

“I don’t trust doctors. Their suggestions has no significance since they don’t experience this. But people with similar symptoms would be of help to give me something valuable to follow.”—— patients

“He doesn’t want to be considered as a patient, so he just pretends to be healthy. Avoiding physical examination and doctor makes sense to him.”——patients' relatives

From interviews above, we found 3 problems that the elderly are faced with:

Trust toward peers, Distrust toward doctors

Due to limited education, Chinese elderly are always frightened of doctors’ complicated description. Hence, they tend to be reserved toward their advices. Also, wardmates’ recovery directly inform them the effects of wardmates’ measures and they would follow it.

Frustration & Lack of Self-discipline

Elderly’s weakened body makes them focus more on avoid being worse instead of being better, for that better means more vigor devotion and this cost them energy. Therefore, with few devotion, they couldn’t establish long-term execution and self-discipline to improve their health sustainably.

Shame from illness and diseases

From what patients’s relatives and doctors have described, Patients are shamed of illness, when others inform them of the illness, they tend to avoid getting medical advice from professional medical services whose diagnosis are authoritative that make them at a loss.

But we noticed some features of their current measures:

Digital Gap

In China, services are turned online and offline service are simplified or cancelled. Smart phones’ interaction is strange to Chinese elderly since they didn’t use it until getting old. They can’t remember the interfaces and this brings them inconvenience.

New Social Contact

Losing social contact, elderly patients reach others in some public places. Take a park as an example, an elderly takes care if anyone’s strange in their body balance to find out someone with similar problems. Then he would reach out and build a connection after encounters.

Media Reliance

In China, elderly are used to note some tips on paper or use calendary as memo, which are shouldered by app. Their accustomed materials & files are presented mainly on papers. Therefore, they have been accustomed to the paper medium

We found that there are some features that ought to be satisfied

To make people have access to what they need, we need to innovate on the medium which was meant to bring them convenience. Also, following 4 concepts should be realized:

New Media

Since they can’t handle smart phones well to access different services. They need to be offered a new user-friendly way to get help. A familiar way to get access is easier to be accepted by them

Shared recognition

People recognize each other’s CVDs by behaviors. To help them more sensitive and build a stronger connection, it’s necessary that we unify some logos or forms that make them connected at sight of the signs.

Collection joy

Due to poverty , Chinese elderly tend to collect something that has no use. Collection means storage and this gives them a sense of safety that when in need in the future they could tackle it with the useless collections. Coupons, tickets and stubs have priority of their collection.

Focus on life & dismiss illness

The process of living a better life has no direct relation to specific illness such as CVDs. As a result, we avoid mentioning illness during the service practices to make them participate actively with no worry.

We designed a new way to make them have access to prevention positively and voluntarily.

As a result, we designed the book to make them have access to different services by papers. A book is kind of like the meme they would record and recall some information and steps. We designed the VI to make patients easily recognize each other by covers of the book when hanging out.

We designed the book to make them have access to different services by papers.

We integrate some necessary services which are helpful to their health into the stamp coupons. People use coupons in exchange for services like the nutritional vegetables package and get stamps in return. Due to the collection property of stamps , they would be promoted to exchange more services and get more stamps. Also , coupons are substitute of prescription that shift their attention from shame and worry of illness to better habits.

Different services jointly make for their fight against CVDs.
